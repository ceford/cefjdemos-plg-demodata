The Jacobite risings were periodic attempts to restore the Catholic Stuarts to the British throne by force of arms following their replacement by the Protestants William and Mary in the Glorious Revolution of 1688.

Jacobitism was a political ideology strongest in Ireland, the Western Scottish Highlands, Perthshire and Aberdeenshire. Pockets of support were also present in Wales, Northern England, the West Midlands and South West England. Risings that led to battles began in 1689, 1715, 1719 and 1745. The battle of Culloden on 16 April 1746 was the last large scale pitched battle fought on British soil.

Jacobitism petered out after the death of Charles Edward Stuart (Bonnie Prince Charlie) in Rome in January 1788.
